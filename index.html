Featured animation

Posterior convergence under HMC sampling. The animation shows how a kernel density estimate of the posterior for the global GDP effect parameter evolves as sampling progresses. The dark curve is the running density computed from the first k post-warmup HMC draws, where k increases monotonically over time. The light curve is the final posterior density computed from all available post-warmup draws and serves as a reference. The moving point indicates the current sample value at iteration k. As k grows, the running estimate fluctuates less and concentrates around the final posterior, illustrating Monte Carlo convergence.
