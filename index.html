Featured animations

Posterior convergence under HMC sampling. The animation shows how a kernel density estimate of the posterior for the global GDP effect parameter evolves as sampling progresses. The dark curve is the running density computed from the first k post-warmup HMC draws, where k increases monotonically over time. The light curve is the final posterior density computed from all available post-warmup draws and serves as a reference. The moving point indicates the current sample value at iteration k. As k grows, the running estimate fluctuates less and concentrates around the final posterior, illustrating Monte Carlo convergence. Transformer ablation: removing residual connections and layer normalization. In this setting, perplexity largely correlates with qualitative performance; nevertheless, prompt-conditioned generation with a fixed prefix (ROMEO:) provides an interpretable view of how training progress manifests in syntactic and stylistic structure. Removing layer normalization and residual connections markedly impedes optimization and degrades generated text, even when perplexity improves steadily early on.
